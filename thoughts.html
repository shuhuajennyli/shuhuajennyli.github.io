12/11/2023

Embark on a journey to the dimly lit staircase of Fine Hall, where your only task is to ascend and descend its fourteen flights of stairs. Leave behind the distractions of earbuds; let your mind remain untouched by the words and melodies of others. You might discover that this simple yet demanding activity leaves your mind both fatigued and remarkably clear, providing the perfect backdrop for introspection. Perhaps, as you climb, you'll catch whispers of inner dialogue among the voices in your head. Similar to the aftermath of a restful night, your thoughts may feel more developed, and your mental state matured.

The tranquility that follows this silent conversation is unfiltered and raw. It emerges after you've pushed yourself, experiencing pain and hardship solely by your own choice. In this endeavor, you're not proving anything to anyone but yourself; the only observer is you. At last, you find yourself in complete control, feeling a reinvigorated sense of what it truly means to be alive.

Kierkegaardian Thoughts

Consider a scenario in which every choice you make is a reflection of your character, permanently etched in your personal history. Each choice you make is written in a script that cannot be erased, offering no escape from your record. Your life is an experiment where you are the subject of a study. All else is a simulation, and laboratory notes on every single movement of your body are recorded and analyzed, whether it is your choice to attend Princeton or lie to your mother about a poor math grade. None of what other people think, how others react, what others do, matters in the end, as it is only your actions that are relevant to the experiment. How would you live your life?

09/05/2023

Technology has transcended mere numbers, and instead presents a plethora of ethical questions that require exploration. From mitigating bias in big data, which demands insights from sociologists, to contemplating the possibility of sentient AI, which delves into consciousness, emotions, and what it means to be human, these challenges call for an amalgamation of diverse disciplines. As a proponent of empathetic problem solving, I believe in thinking beyond the confines of hard technology and embracing the integration of humanistic ideas into scientific pursuits.
